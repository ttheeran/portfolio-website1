Nest

Overview

Nest is a personal savings app designed to help users define financial goals, track progress, and stay motivated along the way. The app combines clean visuals with trust-building features, encouraging users to build healthier financial habits without feeling overwhelmed.

The Challenge

Saving money can feel abstract and discouraging, especially when goals are long-term. During research, users expressed a need for clarity, transparency, and a sense of accomplishment along the journey. My challenge was to create a design that balanced functionality with emotional engagement.

My Role & Process

  • Conducted user research through surveys and competitor analysis to uncover common frustrations with savings apps.
  • Developed user flows mapping out how goals are created, tracked, and celebrated.
  • Sketched and tested wireframes with users to validate navigation and task completion.
  • Designed high-fidelity prototypes in both light and dark mode, incorporating gradients and trust-driven UI patterns.

The Solution

Nestâ€™s design features progress tracking through milestones, clear goal-setting flows, and a minimal interface with visual cues that reinforce confidence. The milestone rewards were particularly well received in testing, as they gave users a reason to stay engaged.

Key Takeaway

This project highlighted the importance of designing for emotion, not just utility. Users felt more motivated when the app acknowledged their progress, proving how UX can play a role in building trust and confidence.
