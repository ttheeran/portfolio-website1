Awesome 80's Music Player App

Overview

The Awesome 80’s Music Player is a retro-inspired app that merges the bold energy of the 1980s with the functionality of modern streaming platforms. Its goal was to create a playful, nostalgic experience that still feels usable today.

The Challenge

Music apps are dominated by minimalist design trends. The challenge here was to stand out with personality while ensuring legibility, usability, and consistency across devices. Too much “retro” risked becoming kitschy or distracting.

My Role & Process

  • Researched 80's design trends including typography, neon color palettes, and visual motifs from posters and album covers.
  • Experimented with bold font pairings, grid systems, and retro-inspired iconography.
  • Prototyped multiple layouts for the track screen and playback controls, testing readability against strong color and pattern choices.
  • Iterated high-fidelity mockups in both dark and light themes, keeping accessibility top of mind.

The Solution

The final app pairs vibrant gradients, bold typography, and subtle retro details with clean modern UI structures. The player screen, in particular, became a focal point — balancing fun visual expression with simple navigation so users could focus on the music.

Key Takeaway

This project taught me how to balance creativity with usability. Nostalgic design elements can enhance user delight, but they must be carefully controlled so that function always remains front and center.
